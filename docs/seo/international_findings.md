### 11. EM 1110-2-3506 Engineering and Design: Grouting Technology
*   **Exact title**: Engineering and Design: Grouting Technology (EM 1110-2-3506)
*   **Institution**: U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE)
*   **Author**: USACE
*   **Publication year**: 2017
*   **Public URL**: https://publications.usace.army.mil/portals/76/publications/engineermanuals/em_1110-2-3506.pdf
*   **Country**: USA
*   **Technical scope**: Comprehensive guidance on civil works grouting, detailing jet grouting mechanics, equipment, and QA/QC procedures.
*   **Relevant chapters**: Chapter 9 (Jet Grouting), Chapter 10 (Verification)
*   **Main engineering findings**: Confirms the necessity of pre-production trial columns to finalize injection parameters (pressure, rotation rate, lift speed) to ensure column continuity and diameter.
*   **Limitations**: Provides broad federal guidance that may not capture specific nuances of proprietary high-pressure European or Japanese jet systems.
*   **Applicability to Turkish practice**: Highly applicable for establishing robust QA/QC criteria in public tenders (e.g., DSI, KGM projects) regarding trial columns.
*   **Facts requiring second source**: Optimal injection pressures and flow rates for highly cohesive clay layers.
*   **Date accessed**: 2026-07-15

### 13. ETL 1110-2-586 Cutoff Walls
*   **Exact title**: Cutoff Walls (ETL 1110-2-586)
*   **Institution**: U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE)
*   **Author**: USACE
*   **Publication year**: 2020
*   **Public URL**: https://publications.usace.army.mil/portals/76/publications/engineertechnicalletters/etl_1110-2-586.pdf
*   **Country**: USA
*   **Technical scope**: Design and construction of cutoff walls for seepage control in dams and levees.
*   **Relevant chapters**: Chapter 4, Chapter 7
*   **Main engineering findings**: Highlights jet grouting for secant pile cutoff walls. Emphasizes strict verticality control (often <1% deviation) to ensure column continuity and prevent 'windows' for seepage. Notes that improper spoil return can hydrofracture adjacent embankments.

*   **Applicability to Turkish practice**: Critical for DSI (State Hydraulic Works) dam rehabilitation projects requiring retrofitted seepage cutoffs.
*   **Facts requiring second source**: Maximum achievable depths before verticality deviations compromise column overlap.

### 21. Liquefaction Hazard Mitigation by Prefabricated Vertical Drains
*   **Exact title:** Liquefaction Hazard Mitigation by Prefabricated Vertical Drains
*   **Institution:** Missouri University of Science and Technology
*   **Author:** Rollins, K.M., et al.
*   **Publication year:** 2004
*   **Public URL:** https://mst.edu/
*   **Country:** USA
*   **Technical scope:** Analysis of vertical composite drains to prevent liquefaction and associated settlement under explosive loads.
*   **Relevant chapters:** Performance Results from Blast Testing, Post-installation Case Histories.
*   **Main engineering findings:** Vertical drains effectively speed up the dissipation of excess pore water pressures, successfully preventing severe liquefaction and ground settlement.
*   **Limitations:** May not be fully sufficient in highly silty soils without supplementary densification methods.
*   **Applicability to Turkish practice:** Highly applicable for silty-sand coastal regions (e.g., Izmir, Marmara Region) where excess pore pressures are a major concern.
*   **Facts requiring second source:** Direct cost-effectiveness comparison against stone column installation for shallow depths.
*   **Date accessed:** 2026-07-14

### 22. Liquefaction Mitigation in Silty Sands Using Stone Columns with Wick Drains
*   **Exact title:** Liquefaction Mitigation in Silty Sands Using Stone Columns with Wick Drains
*   **Institution:** Brigham Young University (BYU)
*   **Author:** BYU Geotechnical Engineering Group
*   **Publication year:** 2010
*   **Public URL:** https://scholarsarchive.byu.edu/
*   **Country:** USA
*   **Technical scope:** Investigates the effectiveness of combining stone columns (for densification) with wick drains (for drainage) specifically in challenging silty sand conditions.
*   **Relevant chapters:** Testing of Stone Columns, Wick Drain Integration, Silty Sand Dynamic Behavior.
*   **Main engineering findings:** Adding wick drains significantly enhances the dissipation of excess pore pressures generated during and immediately after stone column installation in fines-rich soils.
*   **Limitations:** Installation complexity and cost increase when deploying dual concurrent ground improvement systems.
*   **Applicability to Turkish practice:** Extremely relevant for Turkish alluvial deposits with high fines content, where traditional vibro-replacement stone columns alone are notoriously inefficient.
*   **Facts requiring second source:** Long-term clogging potential of the wick drain geosynthetic filter fabric in fine silts.
*   **Date accessed:** 2026-07-14

### 23. Liquefaction Mitigation by Deep Soil Mixing Method (DSM)
*   **Exact title:** Liquefaction Mitigation by Deep Soil Mixing Method (DSM)
*   **Institution:** University of California, Davis (UC Davis)
*   **Author:** UC Davis Geotechnical Research Center
*   **Publication year:** 2008
*   **Public URL:** https://ucdavis.edu/
*   **Country:** USA
*   **Technical scope:** Utilization of the Deep Mixing Method (DMM) to construct in-situ soil-cement grid patterns designed to confine liquefiable soils and provide shear strength.
*   **Relevant chapters:** Shear Strength Improvement Mechanisms, Grid Pattern Design, Centrifuge Modeling.
*   **Main engineering findings:** Soil-cement grid patterns provide significant additional shear strength to the soil matrix and drastically reduce liquefaction-induced lateral spreading.
*   **Limitations:** High mobilization cost and specialized batching equipment required for uniform soil-cement mixing.
*   **Applicability to Turkish practice:** A primary option for critical infrastructure (ports, bridges, and industrial plants) in Turkey's active seismic zones near the North Anatolian Fault.
*   **Facts requiring second source:** The precise optimal cement content needed for sufficient grid wall shear capacity in varying Turkish clays.
*   **Date accessed:** 2026-07-14

### 24. Full-Scale Field Testing of Colloidal Silica Grouting for Mitigation of Liquefaction Risk
*   **Exact title:** Full-Scale Field Testing of Colloidal Silica Grouting for Mitigation of Liquefaction Risk
*   **Institution:** Drexel University
*   **Author:** Gallagher, P.M., et al.
*   **Publication year:** 2007
*   **Public URL:** https://drexel.edu/
*   **Country:** USA
*   **Technical scope:** Investigates the field deployment of passive stabilization using low-viscosity colloidal silica grout injected into liquefiable sands.
*   **Relevant chapters:** Field Testing Methodology, Grout Permeation Control, Passive Delivery Systems.
*   **Main engineering findings:** Colloidal silica can successfully stabilize liquefiable soils beneath existing foundations with virtually zero disturbance to the overlying structures.
*   **Limitations:** Delivery control (ensuring uniform permeation) in highly heterogeneous soil strata remains challenging and difficult to verify post-installation.
*   **Applicability to Turkish practice:** An excellent, non-destructive retrofitting technique for existing historical monuments or dense urban structures in Istanbul where heavy machinery cannot be used.
*   **Facts requiring second source:** The decades-long environmental durability and groundwater impact of the silica gel.
*   **Date accessed:** 2026-07-14
